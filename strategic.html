These values resonate with the foundational principles of the Universal Web, which aim to create a secure, inclusive, and morally grounded digital environment.

In contrast, our engagement with regions in the West, such as AMER (Americas) and EMEA (Europe, Middle East, and Africa), is considered a secondary extension.     Open Source Licensing empowers communities by bringing them together and enabling collaborative efforts. A common challenge in this model is ensuring the sustainability of the community, particularly in terms of funding and rewarding contributions.

    To address this issue for the 'Universal Web' reference implementation, the UTA will release all sources, code, and generated content under a tailored Community Source License. This license will grant licensed parties the rights to use and distribute the content within their network.

    UAC subscription members automatically receive a Community Source License, which provides them with all the rights typically granted by an MIT license. The goal is to expand the community to include a broad base of users of technical systems. Empowered Society Program

This is the R&D program, known as Labs, which executes multiple Smartup projects aimed at fostering innovation and community development. A Smartup is a collective that democratically creates, owns, and governs technology to advance the development of The Decentralized Autonomous Cloud. Through Labs, these projects receive the necessary resources, guidance, and collaborative environment to thrive, ensuring that each initiative aligns with the broader mission of sustainable and equitable progress. By integrating cutting-edge research and development with community-driven objectives, Labs plays a crucial role in driving forward impactful technological solutions.

From the perspective of the UAC, each project is meticulously defined and mapped to ensure effective dependency management. Contributions to the system, whether in creation, implementation, or testing, are clearly identified and tracked. This holistic approach allows for a comprehensive understanding of how each project component integrates into the larger system. By outlining specific roles and responsibilities, the UAC ensures that all contributions are recognized and aligned with the overarching objectives of the network.

Program management is conducted from the ESP perspective, which orchestrates activities to achieve product development, delivery, and integration. This involves coordinating various tasks and milestones, ensuring that dependencies are managed efficiently, and that all elements of the project progress in a synchronized manner. By leveraging the UAC and ESP frameworks, projects are systematically guided from inception to completion, maintaining transparency, accountability, and alignment with the network's strategic goals.
